The challenge rules define exactly what you need to achieve to pass, and what you must avoid to stay active. The questions below address the most common points of confusion around drawdown measurement, trading day requirements, risk limits, and what happens if a rule is breached.

<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion title="What is the profit target?">
The profit target is **30% of your starting balance**, but it is measured differently on challenge and funded accounts.

- **Challenge pass:** your **equity** must reach the 30% target, and you must complete 7 trading days.
- **Funded payout eligibility:** your **realized profit** must reach the 30% threshold after prior paid payouts are accounted for.

For example:
- \$10,000 account → \$3,000 target
- \$50,000 account → \$15,000 target
- \$200,000 account → \$60,000 target
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What counts as a trading day?">
A trading day is any calendar day on which you place **at least one trade with a notional value of \$1 or more**. You need at least **7 distinct trading days** before you can pass a challenge.

Days are counted separately — trading a large volume on one day does not substitute for trading across multiple days.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How is the daily drawdown limit measured?">
At the start of each UTC day, PolyFundr takes a snapshot of your equity. If your equity drops more than **10% below that snapshot at any point during the day**, the account fails immediately.

For example, if your equity at the start of a UTC day is \$52,000, your account would fail if equity fell below \$46,800 (a \$5,200 drop) at any point that day.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How is the total drawdown limit measured?">
Total drawdown is measured on a **trailing high-water mark** basis. PolyFundr tracks your all-time peak equity. If your equity ever falls more than **20% below that peak**, the account fails.

The high-water mark only moves up — once you reach a new equity high, the drawdown floor rises with it. It never resets downward.

For example, if your account peaks at \$60,000, the total drawdown floor is \$48,000. If you then return to \$60,000 and reach \$65,000, the new floor becomes \$52,000.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What is the max risk per trade?">
The max risk per trade is **5% of your starting balance**. Before every trade is placed, PolyFundr checks the worst-case loss across all your open positions on the same market plus the new trade. If the combined worst-case loss would exceed 5% of your starting balance, the trade is blocked.

For example, on a \$50,000 account, no single setup can risk more than \$2,500.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What is the inactivity limit?">
If **14 days pass without a qualifying trade**, the account fails. The inactivity clock starts when the account is activated and resets every time you place a qualifying trade (at least \$1 notional).

This rule applies to both challenge accounts and funded accounts.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens if I breach a rule?">
The account fails immediately when any rule is breached. You can purchase a new challenge at any time to start again. There is no appeal process for rule violations — the rules are enforced automatically and in real time.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do the same risk rules apply on funded accounts?">
The funded account keeps the same **daily drawdown, total drawdown, max risk per trade, and inactivity** rules. The main difference is the profit target: challenge passing is equity-based, while payout eligibility on funded accounts is based on realized profit.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Are challenge and funded account trades mixed together?">
No. When you pass a challenge, PolyFundr opens a new, separate funded account. Challenge trades and funded trades are never combined — they exist on entirely separate accounts. This means your challenge history has no effect on your funded account balance or rules.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Does withdrawing profits affect my drawdown limits?">
No. Drawdown calculations are adjusted for any payouts already made. Withdrawing your profits will never move you closer to a drawdown breach — the drawdown floors are recalculated to account for funds you have already taken out.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Are there any restrictions on when or what I can trade?">
No. You can trade any market available on PolyFundr at any time — including on weekends, around news events, and at any hour. There are no session restrictions, news trading bans, or holding period requirements. You can hold positions for as long as you like, provided you remain within the inactivity and risk rules.
</Accordion>
