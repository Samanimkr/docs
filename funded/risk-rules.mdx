The risk rules that applied during your challenge carry over unchanged to your funded account. There are no new rules to learn and no hidden restrictions — the same profit target, drawdown limits, max risk per trade, and inactivity limit govern your funded account from the moment it opens.

## Rules at a glance

| Rule | Value |
|---|---|
| Profit target | +30% of starting balance (per payout cycle) |
| Max daily drawdown | 10% of the day's starting equity |
| Max total drawdown | 20% trailing from peak equity |
| Max risk per trade | 5% of starting balance |
| Min trading days | 7 (between payouts) |
| Max inactivity | 14 days |

## How each rule works

### Profit target

Your profit target is 30% of your funded account's starting balance. This resets at the start of each payout cycle. The target is based on **realized profit only** — open positions do not count until they are closed.

| Account size | Profit target |
|---|---|
| $10,000 | $3,000 |
| $25,000 | $7,500 |
| $50,000 | $15,000 |
| $100,000 | $30,000 |
| $200,000 | $60,000 |

### Max daily drawdown

At the start of each UTC day, your equity is snapshotted. If your equity drops more than 10% below that level at any point during the day, your funded account is breached.

<Warning>
  The daily drawdown resets each UTC midnight — not at a fixed clock time local to you. Plan your trading sessions accordingly.
</Warning>

### Max total drawdown

PolyFundr tracks your all-time peak equity. If your equity ever falls more than 20% below that peak, your funded account is breached. The peak only moves upward — once you reach new highs, the floor rises with you.

<Info>
  Drawdown calculations are adjusted for payouts you have already received. Withdrawing profits does not lower your drawdown floor or move you closer to a breach.
</Info>

### Max risk per trade

Before every trade, the platform checks the worst-case loss across all your open positions in the same market plus the new trade. If that combined exposure exceeds 5% of your starting balance, the trade is blocked.

This rule is enforced on the starting balance of your funded account — not on your current equity — so the absolute dollar limit stays constant throughout the payout cycle.

| Account size | Max risk per trade |
|---|---|
| $10,000 | $500 |
| $25,000 | $1,250 |
| $50,000 | $2,500 |
| $100,000 | $5,000 |
| $200,000 | $10,000 |

### Minimum trading days

You must trade on at least 7 distinct calendar days between payouts. A qualifying trading day requires at least one trade with $1 or more in notional value. The 7-day clock resets after each payout is approved.

<Tip>
  Spreading your trades across different days — rather than concentrating them — is a natural way to satisfy this requirement without changing how you trade.
</Tip>

### Max inactivity

If more than 14 consecutive days pass without a qualifying trade, your funded account is breached. The inactivity clock starts when your funded account is activated and resets each time you place a qualifying trade.

## What happens if you breach a rule

If a rule violation is detected, your funded account is closed. You would need to purchase and pass a new challenge to receive a new funded account.

<Note>
  The max risk per trade rule is enforced in real time — trades that would breach it are blocked before they execute. Daily and total drawdown violations are evaluated continuously and will close the account immediately on breach.
</Note>
