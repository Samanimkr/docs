Trades on PolyFundr are simulated. There is no on-chain execution. When you submit a trade, the platform records your position using the current Polymarket price as your entry price. Your shares and potential PnL are calculated from that snapshot. You need an active challenge or funded account to place a trade.

## Before you trade

<Info>You must have an active challenge or funded account to submit trades. If you don't have one yet, visit `/accounts` to purchase a challenge. You can also try the free demo account to get familiar with the trade panel before committing.</Info>

## Placing a trade

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open an event">
    From the dashboard, click any event card to open its event page at `/event/[slug]`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select an outcome">
    The outcomes table lists all tradable markets for the event. Click a row to select it, or click the **Yes** or **No** button directly. This selects the outcome and pre-fills the direction in the trade panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose YES or NO">
    In the trade panel, choose your direction:
    - **YES:** you believe the outcome will resolve YES. Your entry price is the current YES token price.
    - **NO:** you believe the outcome will resolve NO. Your entry price is the current NO token price.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter an amount">
    Type a USDC amount in the **Amount (\$)** field, or use the quick-add buttons (+\$1, +\$20, +\$100) to build up your size. The **MAX** button fills in the maximum risk amount allowed by your challenge rules.

    As you type, the panel shows:
    - **Avg. price:** the entry price in cents.
    - **To win:** the potential gain if the trade closes at 100¢.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the trade">
    Click **Trade · \$amount**. The position is recorded immediately at the latest snapshot price.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How shares and entry price are calculated

When your trade is recorded, the platform computes the following:

| Field | Calculation |
|---|---|
| **Entry price** | Current YES token price (for YES trades) or current NO token price (for NO trades), expressed in cents |
| **Shares** | Amount (USDC) ÷ entry price (as a decimal, e.g. 50¢ = 0.50) |

For example: if you trade YES at 40¢ with \$20, your entry price is 0.40 and you receive 50 shares. If YES resolves to 100¢, those shares are worth \$50, which is a \$30 gain on your \$20.

<Note>Each fill is stored as its own position. PolyFundr does not net or aggregate fills on the same outcome. Submitting two separate YES trades creates two separate positions.</Note>

## Wide spread warning

If the spread between YES and NO prices on a market is wider than 3¢, the trade panel shows a warning. A wide spread means you may see an immediate paper loss on your position until the market tightens. This is normal behavior on lower-liquidity markets and is not an error.

<Warning>Always check the spread before trading. A wide spread on a thin market can make it harder to close at a profit, even if the market moves in your favor.</Warning>

## Closing a position

To close an open position before the market resolves:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find your position">
    On the event page, open the **Positions** panel. Your open positions are listed with their current price and live PnL.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sell the position">
    Click **Sell** next to the position you want to close. A confirmation dialog asks you to confirm the sale.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Sell at market**. The position closes at the latest available price and is marked as closed in your portfolio.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You can also close positions from the **Portfolio** page at `/portfolio`, which shows all your open and closed positions across every event.

## What requires an active challenge

The trade button is only active when you have an active challenge or funded account. If your account is not active, the button shows **Buy challenge to start trading** and directs you to `/accounts`.
