PolyFundr runs on a simple three-stage flow: you buy a challenge, prove your trading ability on live Polymarket prices, and receive a funded account the moment you pass. There is no second evaluation phase, no waiting period, and no hidden steps between passing and getting paid.

## Step 1 — Challenge

The challenge is a simulated trading account. You pay a one-time fee to open one, then trade live Polymarket markets within a defined set of rules. The goal is to hit a **30% profit target** on at least **7 distinct trading days**, without violating any risk limit.

### What you need to hit

| Rule | Requirement |
| :--- | :--- |
| Profit target | +30% of starting balance |
| Minimum trading days | 7 days with at least one qualifying trade (\$1+ notional) |

You pass the instant your equity reaches the profit target **and** you have met the minimum trading days requirement, with no active rule violations.

### What you must not breach

| Rule | Limit |
| :--- | :--- |
| Max daily drawdown | 10% of the day's starting equity |
| Max total drawdown | 20% trailing from your highest equity ever reached |
| Max risk per trade | 5% of your starting balance |
| Max inactivity | 14 days without a qualifying trade |

<Warning>
  Breaking any one of these limits ends the challenge immediately. Each limit is evaluated continuously — not just at the end of the day.
</Warning>

### How the limits are measured

**Daily drawdown** is calculated from a snapshot taken at the start of each UTC day. If your equity drops more than 10% below that snapshot at any point during the day, the account fails.

**Total drawdown** tracks your all-time peak equity. If your equity ever falls more than 20% below that peak, the account fails. The floor rises with you — once you reach new highs, the trailing floor moves up.

**Max risk per trade** is checked before every trade is accepted. If the worst-case loss across your open positions on the same market plus the new trade would exceed 5% of your starting balance, the trade is blocked.

**Inactivity** is measured from activation. The clock resets with every qualifying trade. Going 14 consecutive days without a trade fails the account.

## Step 2 — Funded account

Pass the challenge and a new funded account at the same starting size opens automatically. There is no second phase, no identity check, and no manual review step.

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="Same account size" icon="scale">
    Your funded account starts at the same balance as the challenge you passed — \$10k, \$25k, \$50k, \$100k, or \$200k.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Same risk rules" icon="shield-check">
    The same daily drawdown, total drawdown, max risk per trade, and inactivity rules apply to your funded account.
  </Card>
</Columns>

Your challenge trades and funded trades are completely separate. The funded account starts with a clean slate.

## Step 3 — Payout

Once your funded account reaches the payout threshold, you can request a payout directly from your dashboard.

<Note>
  Challenge passing is based on **equity** reaching the target. Funded payout eligibility is based on **realized profit** after prior paid payouts. Open positions do not count until they are closed.
</Note>

### Payout details

- **Profit split:** 90% to you, 10% to PolyFundr.
- **Payout method:** USDC on Polygon to the destination wallet address you enter on that request.
- **Current request cap:** Up to \$1,000 gross per payout request.
- **Pending payouts:** Any amount you have requested but not yet received is reserved against your payout availability until the request is approved or rejected.

### Example

On a \$50,000 funded account, the payout threshold is \$15,000 in realized profit. Once you are eligible, the current maximum request is \$1,000 gross. At a 90% split, that request pays \$900 to you.
