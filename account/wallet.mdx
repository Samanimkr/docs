Every PolyFundr account includes a wallet. When you sign in for the first time, Privy automatically creates an **embedded wallet** for you — you do not need to install a browser extension or manage a seed phrase. You can also connect your own external wallet if you prefer to use one you already have.

Challenge fees are paid in **USDC on Polygon**, and payouts are sent in USDC on Polygon to the destination address you provide.

## Your embedded wallet

Privy creates an embedded wallet the moment your account is set up. This wallet is linked to your PolyFundr account and is available immediately after sign-in.

<Info>
  The embedded wallet is used for the challenge payment flow. When you start a challenge, Privy opens a funding sheet where you can pay by card or by sending USDC from any wallet.
</Info>

## Connecting an external wallet

If you prefer to use an external wallet (such as MetaMask or Coinbase Wallet), you can connect it when prompted during the sign-in or checkout flow. Privy supports browser extension wallets and WalletConnect-compatible wallets.

## Challenge fees and USDC on Polygon

All challenge fees are denominated in **USDC on Polygon**. When you purchase a challenge, the payment is directed to PolyFundr's treasury address. Make sure you are sending USDC on the Polygon network — not on Ethereum mainnet or another chain.

The one-time challenge fees are:

| Account size | Fee      |
| ------------ | -------- |
| \$10,000      | \$125     |
| \$25,000      | \$300     |
| \$50,000      | \$500     |
| \$100,000     | \$900     |
| \$200,000     | \$1,500   |

<Warning>
  Do not send funds on the wrong network. Transfers sent on Ethereum mainnet or other chains cannot be automatically recovered. Always confirm you are using **Polygon** before sending.
</Warning>

## Receiving payouts

When you request a payout from a funded account, you must provide a **destination wallet address** that can receive USDC on Polygon. This address is required on every payout request — PolyFundr does not save a default destination. The current maximum request is \$1,000 gross per payout.

You can view your challenge status, account history, and payment records on the **Accounts** page at [polyfundr.com/accounts](https://polyfundr.com/accounts).

<Tip>
  Double-check your destination address before submitting a payout request. Payouts are sent directly to the address you provide, and transactions on Polygon cannot be reversed.
</Tip>
