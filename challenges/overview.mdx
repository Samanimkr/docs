## Challenge tiers

| Account size | One-time fee | Profit target (30%) | Daily drawdown limit (10%) | Total drawdown limit (20%) | Max risk per trade (5%) |
| :-- | :-- | :-- | :-- | :-- | :-- |
| \$10,000 | \$125 | \$3,000 | \$1,000 | \$2,000 | \$500 |
| \$25,000 | \$300 | \$7,500 | \$2,500 | \$5,000 | \$1,250 |
| \$50,000 | \$500 | \$15,000 | \$5,000 | \$10,000 | \$2,500 |

\$100,000 and \$200,000 accounts are not on direct sale right now. They are awarded through competitions and giveaways, and run on the same percentage-based rules (for example a \$30,000 / \$60,000 profit target).

<Info>
All percentage-based rules are the same across every tier. The dollar figures above are derived from the starting balance for each account size.
</Info>

## What is the same on every tier

- Live Polymarket prices
- One-step evaluation
- Same percentage-based rules
- No KYC required to start
- No challenge expiry date
- Immediate activation after payment confirmation

## What happens when you pass

Pass the challenge and PolyFundr automatically opens a funded account at the same size.

- There is no second phase.
- The funded account carries the same drawdown, max-risk, and inactivity rules.
- You keep 90% of funded payouts.
- Payouts are sent in USDC on Polygon.
- Funded payouts use a progressive cap ladder (\$1,000, then \$2,000, then
  \$5,000+), and each payout is also limited to half of your new eligible profit
  since the last one, with a 7-day cooldown. See [Funded payouts](/funded/payouts).

</Tip>
